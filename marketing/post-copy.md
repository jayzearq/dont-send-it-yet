## Reddit-Safe Comment Templates

Use these only when directly relevant. Lead with help, not the link.

```text
If the urge feels really intense, I would not make the decision while your body is still activated. A useful test is: "What am I hoping they will say back, and what will I feel if they do not reply?"

If the answer is reassurance or relief, wait ten minutes and write the message somewhere else first.
```

```text
No-contact slip-ups are common. The important part is not turning one text into a verdict about your healing. Restart the boundary and look at what triggered the urge: time of day, social media, loneliness, guilt, alcohol, etc.
```

```text
Something that helps is separating the motive:

- comfort
- closure
- control
- connection
- guilt

If it is comfort or control, sending usually keeps the loop open.
```

## Indie Hackers / LinkedIn Launch Post

```text
I launched a tiny web MVP called Don't Send It Yet.

It is a regret-prevention tool for the moment when someone is about to text an ex and already suspects tomorrow might hurt.

The flow is intentionally simple:
- paste the draft
- get a regret score
- start a 10-minute cooldown
- choose whether to save, delete, or send
- optionally start a no-contact reset

The product decision I care about most: it is not an AI relationship coach. It does not try to replace a person. It is a behavioral pause.

Would love feedback on whether the emotional flow feels supportive without being heavy.

https://dont-send-it-yet.onrender.com
```
